How to internally escalate privileges

In order to change an existing user to an admin, an admin must navigate to the ‘Add Admin’ page located on the sidebar. I entered the wrong data. What do I do now?

After entering and submitting any wrong data into the table, you can do the following: If you changed a field of an existing rat, just navigate to the ‘Edit Records’ tab located in the sidebar and change whichever fields that are incorrect. If you added a rat and wished to remove it, you can either report the death of the created rat by accessing the ‘Report Death’ tab located in the sidebar, or edit the record and leave each field blank.

Why can’t I edit records?

The abilities of a regular user are only to view and search the database. An administrator, on the other hand, can Add, Edit, Remove, and perform every other action to the database. A normal user can access each of these pages, but their changes made within each page will not alter the records within the database.

A rat died and i need to report it. How do i do that?

If you need to report a rat’s death then you should navigate the sidebar and click on the report death tab of the sidebar. After clicking on the report death tab, enter the rat’s sex and number and after doing that enter the date the rat died and how the rat died into input fields that show up. After entering those details click the ‘report’ button. After doing that a Modal will pop up and ask if you are sure the data you entered is correct. If you are sure click ‘yes’ otherwise click ‘no’. If ‘no’ is selected then the modal will close and you will be given a chance to reenter the data correctly.

A rat litter has been born and I need to report it! How do I do that?

If you need to report a litter then look at the sidebar and find the tab that says ‘Report Litter’. Once you find that click that tab. It will take you to a page where you can report a rat’s litter. You will see two boxes to enter the Sire and Dam of the litter. There will also be a dropdown menu to report whether or not the litter has one or more rats with a defect present. If so select ‘yes’ otherwise select ‘no’. Once you are done click the ‘Submit’ button. After doing that a Modal will pop up and ask if you are sure the data you entered is correct. If you are sure click ‘yes’ otherwise click ‘no’. If ‘no’ is selected then the modal will close and you will be given a chance to reenter the data correctly.

I need to look up the information for a specific rat, what do I do?

In order to look up a specific rat in the database you should navigate the sidebar of the website and find the tab that says ‘Search’ and click it. This will bring you to a page that displays every rat in the database in a table format. In order to find a specific rat in the table there will be a dropdown menu and a text input field. Clicking on the dropdown will prompt you to select the sex of the rat that you want to search. In the text field you should input the rat’s number. Once both the text and dropdown have been selected you must click the button that says ‘Search. After clicking ‘Search’ the table will filter out any rats that do not match the details you input. And you will have your rat and its associated data.

A rat has been transferred! I need to report it, how do I do that?

In order to report when a rat has been transferred you must navigate the sidebar to the tab that says ‘Report Transfer’. Once found you must click on it. When you are on the page you are presented with a drop down menu and a text box. Click on the drop down and select the rat’s rex and input the rat’s number then click the ‘Report’ button. After doing that a Modal will pop up and ask if you are sure the data you entered is correct. If you are sure click ‘yes’ otherwise click ‘no’. If ‘no’ is selected then the modal will close and you will be given a chance to reenter the data correctly.

We need to get a bunch of breeding pairs together. How do I do that?

In order to generate breeding pairs for a rat you must navigate the sidebar and find the tab that says ‘Generate Breeding Pairs’ and click on it. When you are on the page you are presented with a drop down menu and a text box. Click on the drop down and select the rat’s rex and input the rat’s number. There will be a checkbox that specifies if you want to swap the breeding pair the rat is currently in, it is checked by default. After all of the information has been submitted you click the submit button in order to generate the potential pairs. The best pairs will be at the top of the table that is generated, but all are viable.

Can I trust the breeding pairs that are given to me?

The algorithm that was developed by our team is 100% guaranteed to not result in rats that would cause incest within three generations.

A rat has been selected to join the colony and needs to be added to the database. How do I do that?

In order to add a rat to the database you need to navigate the sidebar and find the tab that says ‘Add Rat‘ and click on it. Once you are on the page you are presented with inputs for the rat’s sex, their birthdate, their sire, their dam, the date they were weaned, the date they were added into the colony, and whether or not they are currently in an experiment. Input all of the associated data in the appropriate fields. After you have correctly input the data, click the add button to add the rat.

I accidentally input the wrong data when adding a rat. How do I change it?

In order to add a rat to the database you need to navigate the sidebar and find the tab that says ‘Edit Record ‘and click on it. Once you are on the page you are presented with a drop down menu and a text box. Click on the drop down and select the rat’s rex and input the rat’s number. Then click the ‘Continue’ button. You will be presented with multiple fields that can be edited. These fields include birthdate, date of last pairing, date of last litter, date weaned, number of litters, number of pairings, date added to colony, number of litters with defects, if the rat is in an experiment, the rat’s sire, and the rat’s dam. Input the fields you wish to edit. After doing that a Modal will pop up and ask if you are sure the data you entered is correct. If you are sure click ‘yes’ otherwise click ‘no’. If ‘no’ is selected then the modal will close and you will be given a chance to reenter the data correctly.

I need to get a view of a rat’s family tree. How do I do that?

In order to get a view of a rat’s family tree, navigate the sidebar and find the tab that says ‘Search’ and click on it. Once on the associated page enter the rat’s sex and number into the associated drop down and text box. Once the data has been input click the ‘Make Tree’ button. Then the family tree of the rat going back to its grandparents of both its sire and its dam will be displayed.

What is the tree that I see on the dashboard? Is it accurate?

The Family Tree feature has been added to help the user to visually understand the relationship between each rat within the database, and yes, it is accurate.

Why are there gaps in the number of the rats?

The data we received was originally incomplete, so there are gaps in the numbers assigned to rats. Any added data through the program should not cause a gap in the number of rats.
